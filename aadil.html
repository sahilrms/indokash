About

Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder is a recognised research and development professional in the field of horticulture in Kashmir Valley providing Consultation on Contemporary and advanced methods. Counted among the leading professionals working in association with the Department of Horticulture-Kashmir for the establishment of high-density apple orchards. For over a period of decade, he has been actively working in the capacity of the Director, Chief Operation Officer of HDP Implementation Company recognised by NAFED and Horticulture Department of Kashmir. He has a vast experience in Orchard development and Management specializes in the studying soil properties, Plant life assessment, environmental assessments, nutrition management. Currently, he is acting as the Director and Head of Research and Development Department at Indokash Horticulture Technologies India Private Limited. After pursuing the post-graduation from the Department of Earth and Environmental Science at Kashmir University, Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder had acquired two years of research experience in Hydrology. After years of research and studies, Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder received the Doctorate of Philosophy (PhD) from K.S.K.V. Kachchh University in Bhuj, Gujarat, from the Department of Earth and Environmental Science in 2022. Further, Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder has one year and three months of research experience as a Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) on groundwater hydrology from the University of Kashmir in 2011 to January 2013 and Senior Research Fellowship at the Institute of Seismological Research, February, 2015 to February, 2017. Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder has an extensive Travel experience to Italy, Netherlands, Greece, and Poland to study the latest development in technology and practices in the field of horticulture and has pursued various Refresher Courses on nutrition, plant pruning, and training management with a nutrition expert at Tradecorp Rovensea Next. Dr. Aadil Hussain Padder has participated in the various Professional National and International Conferences, Seminars and training workshops on Isotope Hydrology (DST and SERC Sponsored) at the National Institute of Hydrology, Roorkee. Participated in the 9th International Congress on the Jurassic system as a field delegate in the field excursion in the Kachchh Basin from December 29 to January 4, 2014, sponsored by the University of Rajasthan and DST, Government of Rajasthan. Mining and Mineral Industry in Kutch: Prospects, Opportunities, and Threats 15–February 2015. DST sponsored a field training workshop on sedimentation, tectonics, and quaternary landforms of the Zanskar Valley (August 25–September 8), 2015. Training Workshop on "Active Fault Mapping and Paleo-Seismology," October 4–12, 2015 (Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur). Gujarat Science Congress 2016 Challenges for Science and Technology Education: Preparing for a Sustainable Gujarat (6th–7th February 2016) Brainstorming session on Quaternary Geology of Western India: Present Status and Future Perspective (8 February 2016). Right now, his research interests are geomorphological correlation with horticulture and agriculture inputs, with major emphasis on geochemistry, its correlation with water-soluble elements, its provenance, and the impact or contribution of anthropogenic factors within horticulture inputs. Soil moisture on a lithological constraint basis, plant training, and pruning management
